Standard Diagnostic Protocols for Initial Assessment

History of Present Illness (HPI) Protocol:
- Onset: When did symptoms start? (sudden or gradual)
- Location: Where exactly is the symptom?
- Duration: How long does it last?
- Character: What does it feel like? (sharp, dull, throbbing, burning)
- Aggravating factors: What makes it worse?
- Relieving factors: What makes it better?
- Timing: Is it constant or intermittent?
- Severity: On a scale of 1-10

Past Medical History Protocol:
- Chronic conditions: diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, lung disease
- Previous surgeries and dates
- Hospitalizations and reasons
- Trauma or injuries

Medication History Protocol:
- Current medications (prescription and OTC)
- Medication allergies and reactions
- Adherence to prescribed medications
- Recent medication changes

Social History Protocol:
- Tobacco use: status, duration, quantity
- Alcohol use: frequency, quantity
- Occupation and exposures
- Living situation and support system
