Treatment Guidelines for Common Conditions

GENERAL TREATMENT PRINCIPLES
============================

Important Note: These are general guidelines only. All treatment decisions must be made by qualified healthcare professionals based on individual patient assessment.

HEADACHE TREATMENT GUIDELINES
============================

Tension-Type Headache:
- Acute treatment: Over-the-counter analgesics (acetaminophen, ibuprofen)
- Non-pharmacological: Rest, hydration, stress reduction
- Prevention: Regular sleep schedule, stress management, ergonomic adjustments
- When to seek care: Frequent headaches (>15 days/month), severe pain, medication overuse

Migraine:
- Acute treatment: Triptans (prescription), NSAIDs
- Non-pharmacological: Dark quiet room, cold compress, caffeine (in moderation)
- Prevention: Identify triggers, preventive medications if frequent
- Red flags: New onset after age 50, neurological symptoms, thunderclap onset

RESPIRATORY CONDITIONS
======================

Upper Respiratory Infection:
- Symptom management: Rest, hydration, honey for cough
- Medications: Decongestants, antihistamines, cough suppressants as needed
- Antibiotics: Only for bacterial infections (not viral)
- When to seek care: Fever > 101°F for >3 days, difficulty breathing, chest pain

Acute Bronchitis:
- Symptom management: Rest, fluids, humidified air
- Medications: Cough suppressants, bronchodilators if wheezing
- Antibiotics: Usually not needed (viral in most cases)
- Seek care: Persistent fever, difficulty breathing, bloody sputum

GASTROINTESTINAL CONDITIONS
===========================

Acute Gastroenteritis:
- Hydration: Oral rehydration solutions, clear fluids
- Diet: BRAT diet (bananas, rice, applesauce, toast) as tolerated
- Medications: Anti-emetics for nausea, anti-diarrheals with caution
- Red flags: Bloody stools, severe pain, inability to keep fluids down

GERD (Acid Reflux):
- Lifestyle: Elevate head of bed, avoid late meals, weight loss
- Dietary: Avoid spicy/fatty foods, caffeine, alcohol, chocolate
- Medications: Antacids, H2 blockers, PPIs as needed
- Seek care: Difficulty swallowing, weight loss, chest pain

CARDIOVASCULAR CONDITIONS
=========================

Hypertension:
- Lifestyle: DASH diet, sodium restriction (<1500mg/day), exercise
- Medications: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, calcium channel blockers, diuretics
- Monitoring: Regular blood pressure checks, home monitoring
- Emergency: BP > 180/120 with symptoms (headache, chest pain, vision changes)

MUSCULOSKELETAL CONDITIONS
==========================

Acute Low Back Pain:
- Initial: Rest (limited), ice/heat therapy
- Medications: NSAIDs, muscle relaxants
- Activity: Gentle movement, avoid prolonged bed rest
- Red flags: Bowel/bladder dysfunction, progressive weakness, fever

Arthritis:
- Non-pharmacological: Exercise, weight management, physical therapy
- Medications: NSAIDs, acetaminophen, disease-modifying drugs (prescription)
- Seek care: Joint swelling, severe pain, inability to use joint

DIABETES MANAGEMENT
==================

Type 2 Diabetes:
- Lifestyle: Diet modification, regular exercise, weight loss
- Medications: Metformin first-line, then additional agents as needed
- Monitoring: Regular blood glucose checks, HbA1c every 3-6 months
- Emergencies: Hypoglycemia (sweating, confusion), hyperglycemia with ketones

MENTAL HEALTH
=============

Depression:
- Non-pharmacological: Psychotherapy, exercise, social support
- Medications: SSRIs, SNRIs as prescribed
- Lifestyle: Regular sleep, healthy diet, avoid alcohol
- Emergency: Suicidal thoughts, inability to function

Anxiety:
- Non-pharmacological: CBT, mindfulness, relaxation techniques
- Medications: SSRIs, benzodiazepines (short-term only)
- Lifestyle: Regular exercise, limit caffeine, adequate sleep
- Seek care: Panic attacks, interference with daily activities

EMERGENCY SITUATIONS
===================

When to Seek Emergency Care:
- Chest pain with sweating, shortness of breath, radiating to arm/jaw
- Severe difficulty breathing
- Sudden severe headache (worst of life)
- Stroke symptoms: facial droop, arm weakness, speech difficulty
- Seizure lasting > 5 minutes
- Severe bleeding not controlled
- Head injury with loss of consciousness
- Suicidal or homicidal ideation with plan

MEDICATION SAFETY
=================

General Principles:
- Take medications exactly as prescribed
- Never share prescription medications
- Report side effects to healthcare provider
- Maintain current medication list
- Know your allergies and always inform healthcare providers

Follow-up Care:
- Schedule follow-up appointments as recommended
- Complete full course of antibiotics if prescribed
- Monitor for improvement or worsening of symptoms
- Seek care if symptoms persist or worsen
