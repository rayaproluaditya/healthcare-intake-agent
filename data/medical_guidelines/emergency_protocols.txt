Emergency Medical Protocols for Intake Assessment

Immediate Emergency Situations Requiring 911/EMS:

Cardiac Emergencies:
- Chest pain with sweating, nausea, shortness of breath
- Pain radiating to left arm or jaw
- Cardiac arrest: unresponsive, not breathing

Respiratory Emergencies:
- Severe difficulty breathing
- Choking or inability to speak
- Blue or pale lips or face

Neurological Emergencies:
- Sudden severe headache (worst of life)
- Stroke symptoms: facial droop, arm weakness, speech difficulty
- Seizure lasting > 5 minutes
- Loss of consciousness

Escalation Protocol:

Level 1 - Immediate Emergency:
- Action: Instruct patient to call 911 or go to ER
- Statement: "This appears to be a medical emergency. Please call 911 immediately."

Level 2 - Urgent (Same Day):
- Action: Recommend urgent care or same-day appointment
- Statement: "I recommend seeking medical attention today."

Level 3 - Routine (1-7 days):
- Action: Schedule appointment with primary care provider
- Statement: "Please schedule an appointment with your provider within the next week."
